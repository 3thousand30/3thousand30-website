privacy

Privacy is not a formality for us. It is part of why we build the way we build.

Our desktop apps are local-first by design. Your files, outputs, and settings stay on your machine. We do not run a hidden cloud layer. We do not store your work on our servers. We do not sell your data. We never have.

This page covers the one place we do collect data: this website.

>

analytics

We use Google Analytics to understand how people find and use this site. Which pages are visited, where traffic comes from, how long people stay. Nothing more.

We ask for your consent before enabling it. If you declined or ignored the prompt, Google Analytics is not running. Nothing is tracked.

If you accepted, here is what that means:

// Google receives anonymised usage data about your visit // no personal details, no account, no profile created by us // we do not share or sell this data // you can withdraw consent at any time below

We do not use advertising trackers, retargeting pixels, or any third-party tools beyond Google Analytics.

>

your_apps

If you use any of our Windows apps, none of that activity touches this website or our servers. The apps are fully local. What you process, generate, compress, watermark, or organise stays on your machine. Period.

For AI-enabled apps (BatchGen Text, BatchGen Image): your prompts go to the AI provider you chose and configured. We do not see them. We do not intercept them. You chose the provider. You pay them directly.
